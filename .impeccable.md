## Design Context

### Users
Two audiences, equal weight:
- **AI builders / agent developers** — engineers building autonomous systems who need error resolution infrastructure. They're evaluating Quash as a dependency, not a product. They think in APIs, reliability, and cost-per-token.
- **Domain experts / solvers** — skilled engineers, sysadmins, language experts who want to monetise deep knowledge. They're evaluating Quash as an income stream. They think in hourly rates, passive income, and credibility.

Both arrive at the landing page cold. Both must immediately see themselves.

### Brand Personality
**Austere. Intelligent. Rebellious.**

The concept is radical — AI should pay humans, not replace them. The design should be the opposite of radical: calm, precise, inevitable. The idea sells itself. The design just needs to not get in the way, then occasionally surprise.

Emotional goal: within 5 seconds, the visitor should feel "this is the future of work" — not through hype, but through quiet confidence.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Theme**: Dark-only. Editorial. Like a financial terminal crossed with a literary journal.
- **Typography**: Lora (serif) + DM Mono — keep. Excellent pairing. Lora gives gravitas, DM Mono gives precision.
- **Color**: Forest green `#3D7A5A` accent — restrained. Never neon. Never gradient.
- **Layout**: Border-divider structure, left-aligned, asymmetric. Newspaper-column sensibility.
- **Anti-references**: No crypto/web3 hype. No neon glows. No "revolutionary." No token animations. No aggressive CTAs. The concept is ambitious; the design is understatement.
- **References**: Stripe (infrastructure credibility), Are.na (austere intelligence), Linear (precision tooling), early Notion (editorial calm).

### Design Principles
1. **Subtract, don't add** — every element must justify its existence. Decoration is a failure of confidence.
2. **Let the concept be radical, not the design** — the idea (AI paying humans) is provocative enough. The visual language should be calm, credible, inevitable.
3. **Both sides see themselves** — agents and experts must each find their moment on the page. Never let one audience feel the page wasn't written for them.
4. **Numbers earn their weight** — any metric shown must be real or clearly illustrative. Fake vanity metrics destroy trust with the exact audience this serves.
5. **Consistency is credibility** — STX not USD. Correct API responses. No dead links. The technical audience will notice every inconsistency.
